Pro Rules

This document is a summary of our Pro rule set, match times, scoring and illegal techniques.

Pro Sport Grappling Gi Rules

General Pro Sport Gi rules are based loosely on the IBJJF rule set but with modifications. In Pro Rules advantages are replaced with a single point which is awarded when a submission technique or positional advancement is nearly achieved and has to be defended. All positional points are awarded following 3 seconds of positional control. Back mount position is deemed achieved when chest to back contact is stabilised for 3 seconds and leg control is present, hooks are NOT required for back mount to be scored. Scoring feet positions for back mount are hooks in, leg triangle, body triangle or feet on hips. Where possible the match will be allowed to continue even if on or just outside the safety area. All takedowns, sweeps etc will continue to be scored until the referee calls for a stop.

Cash Prize Adult Absolute Divisions Gi and NoGi

Our Pro Sport adult absolute divisionsare by pre-registration only as per our other Gi and NoGi divisions. You must have entered one of our other Gi or NoGi divisions to qualify for the absolute division (a podium finish is not required). Cash prizes are only awarded for 1st place and there needs to be a minimum of 4 competitors in that division for prize money to be paid out. Any absolute divisions with less than 4 competitors can still go ahead but no cash prize will be awarded. Pro Sport Absolute Divisions include all juvenile, adult and masters competitors in the SAME bracket.

Our Gi and NoGi Absolute divisions are based on 3 ability levels. Please note that all competitors can choose to move up a level if they want a bigger challenge but you cannot move down a level. E.G a Blue belt can choose to enter the advanced level if they wish but cannot compete down in the Novice level. If you choose to move up a level please be aware that you will also be adopting the higher level legal techniques.
